Kanye West

Kanye West is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and fashion designer. He was born on June 8, 1977 in Atlanta, Georgia, but grew up in Chicago, Illinois. Kanye West is known for his influential contributions to hip-hop music, as well as his controversial public persona and outspoken opinions on a variety of topics. He has released multiple critically acclaimed albums, including "The College Dropout," "Late Registration," and "My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy," and has won 22 Grammy Awards throughout his career. In addition to his music career, Kanye West has also been involved in various business ventures, including his Yeezy fashion line and his short-lived presidential campaign in 2020.

"Runaway" is a 2010 short film directed by Kanye West, featuring music from his album "My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy" and starring West and model Selita Ebanks. The film tells the story of a phoenix who falls to Earth and is taken in by West's character, exploring themes of love, redemption, and artistic expression. It received praise for its stunning visuals, but also criticism for its portrayal of women and West's ego.
